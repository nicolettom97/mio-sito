About me

I am an Electronic Engineer, a graduate of the University of Padua, with a strong background in electronics.

I pursued a Master's degree in Electronic Engineering, followed by a Ph.D. focused on semiconductors for renewable energy.

My research has led to multiple publications in peer-reviewed journals and participation in international conferences, allowing me to engage with the global scientific community.

I value teamwork and collaboration as key drivers of innovation and efficiency.

Currently, I am part of Infineon Technologies, contributing to the development of sustainable and digital solutions through decarbonization and digitalization.

What I do

    Microelectronics Reliability 2025 Role: Author

  1. Hail Damage Investigation in Heterojunction Silicon Photovoltaic Modules: A Real-World Case Study

    IEEE Journal of Photovoltaics 2025 Role: Author
    DOI: 10.1109/JPHOTOV.2025.3539292

      This study investigates hail damage to heterojunction silicon photovoltaic modules using techniques like electroluminescence (EL), infrared imaging, and voltage measurement. The research identifies latent damage and hot spots caused by hailstorms and provides insights into the mechanical resistance of solar panels, offering guidance for standards related to durability and long-term reliability in extreme weather conditions.


  1. TCAD Modeling and Simulation of Dark Current-Voltage Characteristics in High-Periodicity InGaN/GaN Multiple-Quantum-Wells (MQWs) Solar Cells

    IEEE Journal of Photovoltaics 2024 Role: Author
    DOI: 10.1109/JPHOTOV.2024.3366710

      This study presents a TCAD-based simulation and modeling of the dark current-voltage (I-V) characteristics in high-periodicity InGaN/GaN multiple-quantum-well (MQW) solar cells. The analysis focuses on identifying the key physical mechanisms governing the device's electrical behavior in the absence of illumination. The results provide insights into charge carrier transport, recombination processes, and leakage currents, which are crucial for optimizing MQW solar cell performance.


  1. V-Pits and Trench-Like Defects in High Periodicity MQWs GaN-Based Solar Cells: Extensive Electro-Optical Analysis

    IEEE Transactions on Electron Devices 2024 Role: Author
    DOI: 10.1109/TED.2024.3353711

      This study provides a comprehensive electro-optical analysis of V-pits and trench-like defects in high-periodicity InGaN/GaN multiple-quantum-well (MQW) solar cells. These extended defects can significantly impact the device's electrical and optical performance by altering charge transport and recombination mechanisms. Through detailed characterization, the study elucidates the degradation effects induced by such structural imperfections, offering critical insights for optimizing the design and fabrication of GaN-based solar cells.


  1. Influence of V-Pits on the Turn-On Voltage of GaN-Based High Periodicity Multiple Quantum Well Solar Cells

    IEEE Journal of Photovoltaics 2023 Role: Author
    DOI: 10.1109/JPHOTOV.2023.3311891

      This study explores the structural and electrical impact of V-Pits on GaN-based high-periodicity MQW solar cells. By integrating scanning electron microscopy (SEM), atomic force microscopy (AFM), and photoluminescence (PL) spectroscopy, the work demonstrates how V-Pits influence carrier injection efficiency and turn-on voltage behavior. The research provides valuable insights for optimizing MQW solar cells by engineering nanoscale morphological features to mitigate performance losses.


  1. Degradation of GaN-Based Multiple Quantum Wells Solar Cells Under Forward Bias: Investigation Based on Optical Measurements and Steady-State Photocapacitance

    IEEE Transactions on Electron Devices 2023 Role: Co-Author
    DOI: 10.1109/TED.2023.3272297

      The study investigates the degradation of GaN-based multiple quantum well (MQW) solar cells under forward bias stress. The research provides a detailed analysis of charge trapping dynamics and the effects of electric field redistribution, enhancing the understanding of the long-term reliability of GaN-based photovoltaic devices.


  1. Optically Induced Degradation Due to Thermally Activated Diffusion in GaN-Based InGaN/GaN MQW Solar Cells

    IEEE Transactions on Electron Devices 2023 Role: Author
    DOI: 10.1109/TED.2023.3236915

      This paper investigates thermally activated diffusion processes as a key factor in the optically induced degradation of GaN-based InGaN/GaN MQW solar cells. The study combines temperature-dependent electroluminescence (EL) and deep-level transient spectroscopy (DLTS) to track defect migration and charge carrier recombination changes. The results provide critical insights into diffusion-driven degradation pathways, enabling the development of more robust device architectures for high-efficiency solar applications.

    DOI: 10.1063/5.0076833

The model provides a framework for optimizing MQW structures to enhance solar cell performance.
